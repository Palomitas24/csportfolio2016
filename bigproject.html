Rules of Soccer

As in always sport, there are rules and instructions. These are the rules of soccer:

No Hands

As already explained, players can't touch the ball with their hands or arms. Goalkeepers can touch it or grab the ball with thier hands, because that's thier job, to protect the goal.

Throw-ins

A throw-in is taken when the ball crosses a sideline and leaves the field. The two basic soccer rules for a proper throw-in are to have both feet on the ground and to throw the ball withboth hands over the head.

Corner Kicks and Goal Kicks

A corner kick is taken when the ball leaves the field across the goal line (the end of the field). If the offensive team kicks the ball, the play is restarted with a goal kick. If the defensive team kicks it out, the play is restarted with a corner kick, The goal kick is taken from anywhere inside the goal area box. It can be taken by any player. The ball must leave the Penalty Area before anyone can touch the ball. If the ball doesn't leave the Penalty Area then the kick must retake. The corner kick is taken from the corner nearest to where the ball left the field.

Fouls

The common rule on fouls is "If it looks like a foul, it probably is." A player can't kick trip, jump at, charge, strike, push, hold, or spit at an opponent. Bumping, leaning or going shoulder to shoulder while competing for a ball is not a foul until the hands or elbows come up.

Penalty Kick

A penalty kick results from contact foul or hand ball by the defending team within the penalty area, the large box on either end of the field. The ball is placed on the penalty spot, in front of the center of the goal. All players must remain outside the penalty area and the penalty arc until the ball is kicked. The goalkeeper must have both feet on the goal line until the ball is kicked. If after the ball is kicked, it roubounds of the post or goalie, and stay on the field, the ball is "live" and anyone can play it

Offsides

You can't be offside on a corner kick, goal kick, or throw-in. Also, it isn't a offense for a player to be in an offside position The player must be involved in active play as determined by the referee to be called offside. A player is in an offside position if: he is nearer to his opponents' goal line than both the ball and the second to last opponent.

A flagrant foul is a violent contact with an opponent. This includes hitting, kicking, and punching. Intentional fouls are whn a player makes physical contact with another player with no reasonable effort to steal the ball. It is a judgment call for the officials. Rules Of Hockey

As in all sports, there are rules ans instructions. Here are the rules for hockey:

Hockey's Three Main Rules

Offsides

Offfsides is when any member of the attacking team precedes the puck over the defending team's blue line

Offside (or two-line) Pass

When a player passess the puck from his defending zone to a teammate beyond the red center line.

Icing

When a player shoots the puck across the center red line and past the opposing red line. Icing is not called if the players team is killing a penalty, a teammate of the player shooting the puck touches it before a player from the opposing team, the defending goalie touches the puck first or if the puck travels through the crease on its way to the red line

The Penalties

A team plays shorthanded when one or more of its players is charged with a penalty. However, no team is forced to play more than two players below full strength (six) at any tiime. If a third penalty is assessed to the same team, it is suspended until the first penalty expires. When a penalty is called on a goalie, a teammate serves his time in the penalty box.

The bases form a circuit taht must be completed by a base runner in order to score. The central offfensive action entailing hitting a pitched ball with a wooden or metal bat and run to the bases. Rules Of Baseball

As in all sports, there are rules ans instructions. Here are the rules for baseball:

Starting Play

The visting team bats first while the home team starts out in the field on defense. Each fielder lines up in one of the 9 fielding positions. The first batter steps into the batter's box and prepares to hit the pitch.

Batting

There is a batter's box on both sides of home plate. The batter may choose which side of the plate to hit from, but both of his feet must be inside the box. He attempts to score runs by hitting the ball when it's pitched to him. The batter may continue to hit until he: hits the ball into fair territory, gets 3 strikes, or gets 4 balls.

Strikes

The strike zone is the area between the batter's shoulders and knees. A strike is called by the umpire (the ref) when the batter: fails to swing at a pitch that crosses the plate in the strike zone, swings at a pitch and misses, or hits the ball out of bounds with fewer than two strikes against him. Once he gets 3 strikes, he is out, and the next batter comes up to the plate. A batter can't strike out on a foul ball. If he has two strikes, there is no limit to the number of the fouls balls he is allowed to hit. He can only strike out on a swing and miss or a ball he fails to swing at in the strike zone.

Balls

A ball is a pitch the crosses the plate outside of the strike zone that the batter doesn't swing at. If a batter receives 4 balls, he gets to walk to first base. If a batter swings at a ball outside the strike zone and misses, he is called for a strike.

Runner

The batter becomes a runner when: he hits a ball in fair territory and qruns to first base, he walks after 4 balls, he is hit by a pitch or the catcher drops the third strike (If the batter is callled for his 3rd strike but the catcher misses the ball, the batter may try to beat the ball to first base). A runner can move to the next base if the ball is overthrown or after a wild pitch. A runner may overrun 1st base when he hits the ball as long as he turns out of bounds after he passes the base. Runners may not overrun any other base. If they do and are tagged while they are off the base, they are out.

Scoring

The team that scores the most runs is the winner. A run is scored when a base runner rounds all of the bases be stepping on each one in order from 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and crosses home plate. If the ball is hit over the outfield fence in fair territory, it is considered a home run the batter has a 'free trip' all the way around the bases until he crosses home plate. A Grand Slam is a home run that's hit with the bases loaded (runners on every base). All of the base runners round the bases and go home for a total of 4 points (runs).

Putting Players Out

As already explained, a batter is out if : he hits the ball and it's caught while it's in the air, which is know as a fly ball (even if it's cuaght in foul territor) or when he gets 3 strikes. A runner is out if: he is hit by a batted ball while off a base, a fielder tags him with the ball when he is not touching a base, he runs more than 3 feet out of the baseline to avoid being tagged, a fielder with the ball tags a base at which there is a force play before the forced runner reaches the base, or he runs past the base runner that's ahead of him

Plays

Double Play

When 2 players get out on the same play.

Triple Play

When 3 players get out on the same play.

Force Play

When a batter hits the ball and runs to 1st base, he forces the runner on 1st base to 2nd base. If there's also a runner on 2nd, he must move to 3rd base, and if there's also a runner on 3rd base, he must run to home plate. A runner is out if he is tagged by a fielder before reaching the base he is forced to run to or if a fielder with a ball touches the base before he gets there.

Other Rules

Steals

When a runner runs to the next base without the ball ebing hit, usually before the pitcher pitches the ball. The runner is out if he is tagged with the ball before reaching the base.
